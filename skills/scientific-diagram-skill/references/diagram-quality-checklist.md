# Diagram Quality Checklist

Use this before handing off a research diagram.

## Structure

- The reader can identify input, method, output, and validation in under ten
  seconds.
- Every arrow has a clear direction and crosses as few other lines as possible.
- Repeated blocks use the same size, style, and label pattern.
- Optional or failure paths are visually different from the main path.

## Text

- Labels fit inside boxes without shrinking to unreadable sizes.
- Acronyms are expanded once when the audience may not know them.
- Captions explain what the diagram is for, not every arrow in the diagram.
- Chinese and English labels are not mixed unless the document itself is
  bilingual.

## Visual Style

- Use one accent color for the main contribution.
- Avoid decorative shadows, gradients, and busy backgrounds.
- Check grayscale readability if the diagram is likely to be printed.
- Keep enough whitespace around grouped sections.

## Review Questions

- What is the one thing this diagram should teach?
- Which node would a new reader misunderstand first?
- Can any label be replaced with a shorter technical phrase?
- Does the diagram reveal private data, local paths, names, or organization
  details that should stay out of a public repository?
